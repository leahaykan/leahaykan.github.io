New York City's Disconnected Youth

Out of 30 million American youth between the ages of 16 to 24, one in every seven has been left behind. These 5.8 million teens and young adults are defined as disconnected because they are not in school and not working.

Youth Disconnection in the New York metro area: The New York-Newark-Jersey City metro area has nearly 2.5 million young people ages 16 to 24, out of which 353,000 are disconnected.
